Base Cabinets for Countertop Support Base cabinets are types of kitchen cabinets that present the base for countertops.

They are the most important because they support one of the most important and one of the heaviest items in any kitchen. Therefore, they need to be strong, sturdy, and well-balanced. Standard size base cabinets are 36 inches tall and 24 inches deep. Depending on the manufacturer, you can increase the base cabinets depth up to 27 inches. We strongly encourage you to consider increasing their depth because that is one of the best ways to get more storage space. Base cabinets rest atop a four-inch recessed riser â€“ toe kick.

Wall Cabinets for Beautiful Design Wall cabinets, upper cabinets, or wall-mounted kitchen cabinets. These cabinets have different names but are the same. They contrast base cabinets and position above them.

While base cabinets are important because they support countertops and give you a lot of storage options, wall cabinets are the most important for remodeling processes. Upper or wall cabinets are most visible and carry the most of the looks and functionality of any kitchen. At the same time, wall cabinets also provide much-needed storage space.

Pantry (Tall) Cabinets for More Storage Pantry cabinets or Tall Cabinets. You can call them one or the other but the names mean the same.

Pantry or Tall Cabinets feature full height.They start from the floor and extend to the ceiling. The height of pantry cabinets guarantees the maximum of storage usage. Pantry or Tall cabinets usually range in height from 84 to 96 inches per unit. They give the kitchen a dramatically vertical look and can serve as a broom storage because of their size.
